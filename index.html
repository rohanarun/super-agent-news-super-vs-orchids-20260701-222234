Why computer use — and caching — matter now

The agent shift

Across the industry, assistants are becoming agents once they can perceive state, take actions, and observe results. Analysts describe this as the core difference between chatbots and real agents.

Source: adlibrary.com

Computer use goes mainstream

Major platforms are racing to ship computer-use capabilities, including Google’s Gemini adding native computer control for agents.

Source: blog.google

Cost pressure is real

Agent loops consume large numbers of tokens. Research shows that caching and owned context can dramatically reduce repeated execution cost.

Source: harro.com
